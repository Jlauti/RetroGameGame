## Objective

Define the gameplay and visual contract for Nebula's next ground-and-boundary pass so later implementation can proceed without reopening the battlefield-loop decisions made in planning.

Required outcomes:
- lock the battlefield around structured terrain, projectile-blocking topography, and mixed-density combat pockets
- define how many breakable hazards should support the combo loop without replacing enemies as the primary combat value
- define the role of the side cage, shoulder banks, and breakable shoulder fixtures
- lock the procedural neon environment direction so the team does not wait on modeled ground kits
- produce implementation guidance that can be handed directly to Aino for visual language work and Pekka for runtime/procgen work

## Required Decisions To Capture

- Core loop is `Star Goose terrain`, not sparse distance-highrun.
- Side boundaries are `soft cage + banks`, not active bounce rails.
- Density is `mixed`, not constant-max clutter.
- Environment is `procedural neon`, not modeled-env-first.
- Enemies remain the primary combo-feed.
- Breakable hazards are frequent support targets.
- No fuel/resource economy.
- Ricochet remains bonus play, not the center of the game.
- Ships and enemies remain human-modeled `.glb` assets; environment geometry, materials, and VFX may remain abstract/procedural.

## Contract Deliverables

The contract must define:

- Ground motif roster:
  - traversal-safe valleys
  - ridge lines
  - shoulder ricochet banks
  - side pockets
  - breakable hazard clusters
  - rare hard-gate setpieces
- Spatial rules:
  - what belongs in core lane
  - what belongs in shoulders
  - what belongs at the side cage
- Breakable hazard families:
  - which are destructible
  - which are purely structural
  - which can drop health
  - which only clear space or extend combo pressure
- Density cadence:
  - combat pocket
  - relief lane
  - lane-pressure segment
- Boundary behavior:
  - how the side cage pressures the player
  - what gameplay belongs on the cage itself versus just inside it
- Visual-runtime alignment:
  - what `procedural neon environment` must mean in gameplay terms
  - which environment pieces may stay abstract/systemic
  - which gameplay actors still require human-made 3D models
